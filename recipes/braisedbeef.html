Braised Beef

What Is Braised Beef?

Braising is a cooking method that involves slowly cooking meat in a covered pot with some sort of liquid. In the case of this recipe, chuck roast is braised in a mixture of beef broth and red wine. Braising is similar to stewing, but it requires less liquid.

  • 3 ½ pounds boneless beef chuck roast
  • 5 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 2 ½ teaspoons freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 pound cremini mushrooms, halved
  • 3 medium carrots, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 head garlic, cut in half lengthwise
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 3 sprigs thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire
  • 2 cups beef broth, or more as needed
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients and preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Season beef pieces evenly on all sides with 3½ teaspoons salt, 2 teaspoons pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. Add beef, reduce heat to medium-high and cook, undisturbed, until it releases easily from the skillet and a golden brown crust has formed, about 4 minutes.
  4. Flip pieces and continue to cook until browned on all sides. Remove meat from the pan and set aside.
  5. Return skillet to medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons butter to the drippings in the pan. When butter has melted, add onion, mushrooms, and carrots and cook 5 minutes, stirring often and scraping to release any browned bits from the bottom.
  6. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  7. Stir in wine and scrape to release any more browned bits from the pan.
  8. Return the meat to the pan, along with any accumulated juices, and add thyme, bay leaves, and Worcestershire sauce.
  9. Pour in beef broth—just enough that it comes up about two-thirds of the way up on the beef (do not fully submerge the meat in liquid). Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  10. Cover and place in the preheated oven until the meat is very tender, 2 to 2½ hours.
  11. Remove from oven, serve, and enjoy.
